How can we help new parents in NYC dine out without concerns with their kids?

New York City is well-known for its vibrant restaurant scene, always welcoming new restaurants and cafes. However, this isn't for everyone. Mothers living in New York hesitate to take their children to restaurants. Why might this be? And how can we solve this problem?

Overview

This issue began with a complaint from a user on Reddit. We decided to research the experiences of mothers living in New York, and particularly focused on their experiences of dining out with children.

User Research

After conducting field research at Madison Square Park and a co-design session with a mother of two, we discovered that while NYC is more accessible to new parents than expected, there's still room for improvement. For instance, the places they can visit with their children are limited, and often require a partner's assistance. Additionally, small gestures like someone holding the door for a stroller, or having ample space to park a stroller, significantly contribute to their emotional support.

User Pain Points

We identified four major user pain points through our research data:
1. Mobility issue
2. Non-accessible public restrooms
3. Social pressure
4. Not kid-friendly public spaces (cafes, museums, restaurants, etc.)

Journey Mapping

We created a story about taking a child to a restaurant to map out the user journey of a parent (especially mothers). We identified that the most frustrating part is having to consider various factors while dining with a child in a restaurant that is not kid-friendly. This led us to dig deeper into this aspect.

Usability Testing

#No.1
"I find it inconvenient that the UI expands to the left when selecting a mode. How does the UI look when there are multiple modes?"
#No.2
"How is the color setting for mode changes determined? Are the color changes for each mode applied automatically?"
#No.3
"I'm curious if there's a way to switch back to the regular mode after searching for restaurants in the New Mom Mode. The process of switching back to the regular mode might be inconvenient."
#No.4
"It would be great to have tabs on the restaurant's detail page where, in addition to New Mom reviews, They can also check for information like the availability of high chairs and changing tables for babies. This would allow New Moms to gather more information about the restaurant."
